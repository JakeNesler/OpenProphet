## 3. Component Details

### 3.1 Market Concentration (RSP/SPY) — 25% weight
- Equal-weight S&P has **outperformed cap-weight YTD** (+4.24% vs +0.89%) — a textbook early-broadening signal.
- The RSP/SPY ratio has rebounded off the 2003-low-equivalent area near 0.27 with technical analysts looking for 0.30–0.319.
- **But** YTD outperformance has been narrow and recent — SPY's record on May 13 came on AI/semi rebound, suggesting the mega-cap regime hasn't been fully broken.
- **Verdict:** Early Broadening signal, not yet confirmed.

### 3.2 Yield Curve (10Y–2Y) — 20% weight
- Spread at **+0.48%** as of May 13. 2Y at 3.90%, 10Y at 4.38%.
- Curve was inverted Oct 2022 → Dec 2024, then chopped between positive/negative through Feb 2025, now firmly positive.
- A re-steepened curve typically precedes the broadening phase (small-caps, value, cyclicals get re-rated).
- **Verdict:** Risk-on / Broadening regime support.

### 3.3 Credit Conditions (HYG/LQD proxy) — 15% weight
- HY OAS at **2.79%** — historically tight (long-run median ~5%).
- IG corporate OAS ~80 bp, also tight.
- HYG yielding above 6%, LQD ~4.5%; carry-rich, cushion-thin.
- One source flagged "2007 vibes" — tight spreads with leverage at peak is a vulnerability, not a regime change.
- **Verdict:** Risk-on currently, but no margin of safety. First credit widening would flip this fast.

### 3.4 Size Factor (IWM/SPY) — 15% weight
- IWM trailing 12-mo +44.5% vs SPY +31.9% — a major small-cap year just delivered.
- YTD however IWM 14% vs SPY 17% — small-caps **giving back** relative leadership in 2026.
- **Verdict:** Past rotation peaked; current give-back is consistent with the breadth break shown elsewhere.

### 3.5 Equity-Bond Relationship (SPY/TLT) — 15% weight

- TLT under pressure with 10Y at 4.38%; SPY still printed records on May 14.

- **Verdict:** Inflationary regime characteristics persisting; bonds are not a clean hedge.

### 3.6 Sector Rotation (XLY/XLP) — 10% weight

## 5. Portfolio Posture Recommendations

### Strategic (12–18 month) tilt
- **Don't fight the broadening setup, but don't lever into it.** Curve steepening + RSP/SPY rebound off extremes are the right structural signals; the fact that they coincide with a tactical breadth break means starting positions should be moderate.
- **Overweight small/mid-cap and equal-weight exposure** vs cap-weighted — aligned with the regime the rate cycle is signaling.
- **Underweight concentrated mega-cap tech** at the margin — concentration unwinds tend to be sharp once they accelerate.

### Tactical (1–3 month) overlay
- **Raise cash 10–20%** until either (a) breadth confirms (50DMA back above 60%) or (b) a real flush completes (Uptrend Ratio <10%, capitulation).
- Avoid net-short — credit and curve don't support a contraction call yet.
- Selective defensive longs in **Healthcare and Basic Materials** (the only two sectors in confirmed uptrend per breadth CSV).
- Energy is the contrarian short: overbought + rolling = late-cycle distribution.

### Hedge construction
- Bonds are **not** a reliable hedge in current inflationary tilt — May 13 confirmed this.
- Prefer cash and put spreads on QQQ/SPY for downside.
- Gold or commodity equities as inflation hedge, sized small.

### Re-assessment triggers
- **Confirmation of Broadening:** 50DMA breadth back above 60% AND RSP/SPY through 0.31 AND XLY/XLP turns up → upgrade to risk-on.
- **Confirmation of Contraction:** HY OAS widens through 400 bp OR curve re-inverts OR 8MA breadth breaks 30% → downgrade to risk-off.
- **Inflation re-acceleration:** Two consecutive hot CPI prints would push the call toward Inflationary regime, requiring different hedges.
